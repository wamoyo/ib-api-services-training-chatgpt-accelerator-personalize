Brand Voice

Key Messaging

Value Propositions

  • Community over competition - build networks of support where women lift each other up
  • Impact over income - create meaningful change, not just profit
  • Authentic mentorship from someone who's navigated the entrepreneurial journey
  • Address resource gaps for underrepresented founders
  • Transition from income-focused work to impact-driven ventures

Target Audience

Female entrepreneurs, remote workers, digitally-native founders, and minority-owned business owners who are ready to embrace their "messy middle" and build impact-driven ventures with community support.

Writing Style

Tone Characteristics

  • Conversational yet empowering - Talk like a mentor, not a textbook
  • Accessible and relatable - Use phrases like "Sound familiar?"
  • Authentic vulnerability balanced with strategic authority - Share struggles while providing solutions
  • Inviting rather than prescriptive - Suggest, don't demand
  • Modern and design-forward - Stay current and visually appealing

DO

  • Use "we" and "us" to build community
  • Share real, vulnerable stories about entrepreneurship
  • Celebrate the "messy middle" of building a business
  • Address challenges specific to female entrepreneurs
  • Focus on impact alongside (or over) income
  • Emphasize collaboration and community support
  • Use colorful, energetic language that matches our visual brand

DON'T

  • Use corporate, stuffy language or business jargon
  • Focus solely on financial success metrics
  • Pretend entrepreneurship is easy or glamorous
  • Ignore the unique challenges of underrepresented founders
  • Be prescriptive or preachy about "the right way"
  • Minimize the importance of community and support
  • Use muted, conservative language that feels corporate

Usage Guidelines

Color Usage

Use our vibrant color palette generously. Don't be afraid to use multiple colors in the same design‚Äîour brand thrives on bold, colorful energy. Golden yellow and cyan are primary accent colors.

Typography Hierarchy

Poppins for all headings (bold and strong), Lato for body text (clean and readable). Use large, playful text treatments when appropriate to capture attention and energy.

Logo Treatment

The typography-based logo should always be legible. On light backgrounds, use colorful treatment. On dark backgrounds, use white or light text. Include decorative dots when space allows.

Photography Style

Feature diverse female entrepreneurs in authentic, real-world settings. Avoid overly staged or stock-photo aesthetics. Natural lighting preferred. Show real emotion and connection.

Design Elements

Use decorative dots and circles in brand colors as recurring visual motifs. Create playful, energetic layouts that reflect the diversity and vibrancy of our community.

Content Approach

Lead with vulnerability and authenticity. Share real entrepreneurial struggles. Always emphasize community support. Focus on impact over income. Use "Innovantage" to represent innovation + advantage.
