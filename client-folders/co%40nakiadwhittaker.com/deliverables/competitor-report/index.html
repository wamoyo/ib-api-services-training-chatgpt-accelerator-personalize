Competitor Report for The Innovantage Collaborative

Market Analysis & Weekly Tracking | Prepared by Innovation Bound

Your Market Position

Problem You Solve

Female entrepreneurs, especially women of color, face resource gaps when building businesses—chaotic, guarded, and expensive access to authentic guidance. You provide an integrated ecosystem of process design (KISS Virtual Services), networking and mentorship (VA World Empowerment Circle), conferences (Virtual Allies), and personal coaching to transition founders from income-focused to impact-driven ventures.

Your Target Customers

Remote, digital-first, and minority-owned business founders—primarily female entrepreneurs and women of color—who value authentic community support over generic business advice and seek to scale with delegation and systems.

Your Unique Value

You offer a holistic approach combining consulting, community, education, and peer support in one integrated platform, grounded in lived experience with invisible disabilities and centering underrepresented founders rather than mainstream audiences.

Your Top Competitors

Based on market analysis, here are the businesses competing for your customers:

Happy Black Woman®

happyblackwoman.com

What They Do: Coaching company founded by Rosetta Thurman empowering Black women through personal development and entrepreneurship. Offers courses, digital products, events, retreats, masterminds and coaching.

Target Market: Black female entrepreneurs seeking to create "the life and business of their dreams."

Key Strength: Established brand with $5M+ in revenue, featured in Essence, Black Enterprise, SUCCESS Magazine and Entrepreneur. Strong personal brand recognition.

Market Position: Direct competitor for Black female entrepreneur coaching. Larger scale and revenue, focuses on Black women specifically rather than broader WOC audience.

Sistahbiz

sistah.biz

What They Do: Expert business coaching for Black women entrepreneurs to grow, scale & achieve success with strategic guidance & support. Four types of coaching including revenue planning for inconsistent income.

Target Market: Black female entrepreneurs struggling with inconsistent revenue and seeking to scale their businesses.

Key Strength: Specialized expertise in revenue strategy and financial planning for Black women-owned businesses.

Market Position: Direct competitor for business coaching. More focused on financial/revenue aspects than holistic community approach.

Elaine Lou Cartas

elainelou.com

What They Do: Business and Career Coach for women of color and allies, specializing in helping recovering perfectionist leaders implement systems, improve team dynamics, and simplify workloads.

Target Market: Women of color in leadership roles, especially "recovering perfectionists" seeking work-life balance.

Key Strength: MSN awarded her as top business coach of 2024. Strong positioning around perfectionism and systems implementation.

Market Position: Direct competitor for WOC coaching. Similar focus on systems and delegation, but individual coaching practice vs. community model.

Women Of Color Entrepreneurs (WOCE)

woce.us

What They Do: Helps entrepreneurs work with mentors to review business goals, strategy, and implementation. Provides 1-on-1 technical assistance with subject matter experts.

Target Market: Women of color entrepreneurs needing mentorship and technical business assistance.

Key Strength: Established organization focused exclusively on WOC entrepreneurs with structured mentorship matching.

Market Position: Direct competitor for WOC entrepreneurship support. More formal mentorship structure, less community-driven than your approach.

WBENC (Women's Business Enterprise National Council)

wbenc.org

What They Do: Largest certification organization for women-owned businesses. Hosts WBENC National Conference (June 23-26, 2025 in New Orleans), connects women business owners with corporate buyers and provides Women of Color Program.

Target Market: Women business owners seeking certification, corporate contracts, and large-scale networking opportunities.

Key Strength: Massive scale (thousands attend conference annually), corporate connections, established credibility, dedicated WOC program.

Market Position: Indirect competitor. Much larger and more corporate-focused, but Virtual Allies Conference competes for attention/attendance from female entrepreneurs.

The Virtual Savvy

thevirtualsavvy.com

What They Do: Virtual Assistant Agency supporting female entrepreneurs, creatives, and coaches. Also offers VA training programs and community ("The Virtual Assistant Collective").

Target Market: Female entrepreneurs needing VA support AND women wanting to become virtual assistants.

Key Strength: Dual business model (providing VAs + training VAs), established community platform, focus on female empowerment.

Market Position: Direct competitor for VA services and community. Similar female empowerment focus but more focused on VA industry specifically.

Mentor Her

mentorher.global

What They Do: Six-month mentorship program designed to drive business results through accountability, expert advice, and sales/marketing techniques. Structured weekly/monthly material supported by community.

Target Market: Ambitious women entrepreneurs seeking structured mentorship and community accountability.

Key Strength: International reach (Dublin, London, Global), structured curriculum with community support, proven program model.

Market Position: Direct competitor for mentorship and community. More structured/curriculum-based vs. your networked peer support model.

Global Female Entrepreneurs Summit

Female Founder Space

What They Do: Platform for female founders to connect with global mentors, investors, and like-minded entrepreneurs through annual summit. Addresses unique challenges and opportunities in business world.

Target Market: Female founders seeking global networking, investor connections, and mentorship.

Key Strength: Global reach and focus on investor connections. Recorded sessions and exclusive social networking group.

Market Position: Direct competitor for Virtual Allies Conference. Competes for attendees and sponsors in women entrepreneur virtual event space.
